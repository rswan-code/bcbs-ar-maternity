ARKANSAS CONTEXT — WHY THIS MATTERS

4th highest preterm birth rate · high maternal mortality · rural access gaps — the conditions that make Pyx's engagement model non-optional

Arkansas ranks among the worst states in the country for maternal health outcomes. High preterm birth rates, significant rural transportation barriers, and limited prenatal provider access in many counties mean that reaching a pregnant ARHOME member with a standard reminder call is not enough. Pyx's model — food box milestone incentives + human CSC support + EPDS screening + SDoH navigation — is designed specifically for the conditions Arkansas members face. The program doesn't ask members to engage with a portal. It calls them, meets them where they are, and rewards care actions with something they need. That's why it works where standard outreach doesn't.

The Premise
Food is not the incentive — it is the intervention
Standard incentive programs pair a health action with a gift card or points. The Motivate program is built on a different premise: when the #1 documented barrier is food insecurity, putting food at the door in response to a clinical action isn't incentive theory — it is direct barrier removal. A member who completes an EPDS screening or attests a prenatal visit doesn't receive a thank-you message. She receives fresh produce or pantry staples delivered to her home that week, with no application, no waitlist, and no pickup required.
THE CASE FOR FOOD-FIRST DESIGN

81% of members document food insecurity — it's the #1 barrier

Every enrolled member completes a PRAPARE social needs assessment. 81% report food insecurity as a current, active need. This isn't a background risk factor — it's the household reality in which prenatal appointments, medication adherence, and postpartum visits compete with buying groceries. Motivate responds to that reality directly: complete the clinical action, get food. The box is both the barrier response and the engagement signal.

WHY THIS BUILDS LASTING TRUST

Box arrives → next call answered → next screening completed

When a member receives a produce box the same week she completed her EPDS screening, the message is concrete: Pyx follows through. That trust compounds. The member who got food after her first prenatal visit is more likely to pick up the postpartum visit reminder. Motivate is not just activation — it's the relationship infrastructure that makes every subsequent touchpoint easier.

HEDIS Connection
Every box documents a care action BCBS cannot generate through claims alone
HEDIS Prenatal and Postpartum Care (PPC) measures prenatal visits in the first trimester and a postpartum visit between 7 and 84 days post-delivery. For ARHOME members, those actions are fragile — transportation, childcare, food insecurity, and income instability all compete with every scheduled appointment. The Motivate program directly reduces that competition, and documents what happens when barriers come down.
What Each Box Represents in the HEDIS Record
Box 1 — EPDS
Mental health baseline established. At-risk members flagged for follow-up before a missed visit signals risk in claims data.
Box 2 — Prenatal 1
First prenatal visit documented by Pyx. HEDIS PPC prenatal numerator candidate. Food barrier removed the same week.
Box 3 — Prenatal 2
Prenatal continuity confirmed. Two visits in the HEDIS window — the strongest predictor of a complete prenatal care record.
Box 4 — Live Birth
Birth event triggers postpartum engagement. Pyx tracks the 84-day PPC window — the postpartum visit becomes the next documented action in the member's record.

Talking Points — Motivate Program

  • The Motivate program is not an incentive program in the traditional sense — it is a direct SDoH intervention. When 81% of enrolled members screen positive for food insecurity, the food box is the response to the documented barrier, not a thank-you for doing the right thing. This distinction matters for how BCBS communicates the program and how it shows up in equity reporting
  • Every box in the Motivate program corresponds to a documented clinical milestone — EPDS completion, Prenatal Visit 1, Prenatal Visit 2, live birth. Each delivery is tied to a member ID, a milestone trigger, and a delivery confirmation, creating an auditable record for HEDIS documentation, quality improvement, and ARHOME equity submissions
  • ~396 boxes have been delivered directly into the homes of food-insecure ARHOME members since April 2026 — not referrals or warm handoffs, but food at the door. Mixed produce, pantry staples, and baby essentials, each tied to a documented clinical action. The delivery network is the intervention
  • The four Baby Essentials Boxes are the program's most powerful proof point: four births documented, four boxes delivered, four members now actively tracked in the HEDIS PPC postpartum window. These are the members most at risk of dropping out of care at the most critical moment — and they are still in the program
  • As the April 2026 cohort reaches their due dates over the next 90 days, the Baby Box rate will accelerate. Each new birth event creates an immediate engagement touchpoint — box ships, care team calls, postpartum visit gets scheduled. Motivate is how Pyx converts a labor and delivery event into a documented HEDIS PPC postpartum outcome

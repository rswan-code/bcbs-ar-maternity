Pyx Health is reaching ARHOME pregnant and postpartum members in Arkansas with EPDS mental health screening, milestone-based food box engagement, and SDoH navigation — generating the clinical baselines and care attestations that ABCBS's Special Delivery program needs but cannot produce from clinical encounters alone.

Arkansas has the 4th highest preterm birth rate in the nation. Every enrolled member is navigating pregnancy in a state where food insecurity, rural transportation gaps, and social isolation make attending a prenatal appointment a logistical challenge before it's a clinical one. Pyx closes the gap between the visit and the member's daily reality — and documents it for ABCBS.

WHY EPDS MATTERS TO ABCBS

Perinatal depression is the most common complication of pregnancy — and the one most consistently missed without active screening

The Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale is the clinical gold standard for identifying perinatal depression in pregnant and postpartum women. National data shows that 80% of perinatal depression cases go undetected in routine clinical care. Each EPDS Pyx completes is a clinical data point — screening documentation that feeds into ABCBS's behavioral health quality reporting and surfaces members who need care navigation before they generate a behavioral health crisis claim. For ABCBS, Pyx is the screening layer the prenatal office visit doesn't have time to complete.

Talking Points — Clinical Screening

  • EPDS completion at scale is the primary clinical output of the first stage of the program — every completion is a behavioral health baseline that ABCBS can reference against future BH claims and postpartum visit data
  • Perinatal depression correlates with preterm birth, low birth weight, reduced prenatal visit adherence, and postpartum complications — EPDS screening is not a standalone quality metric, it is a predictor of the birth outcomes ABCBS is trying to improve
  • The SDoH screener completion rate drives the downstream resource connection that the SOW measures at 60% — members who complete SDoH screening create the documented need that Pyx then connects to resources
  • UCLA-3 loneliness baseline is unique to the Pyx model — no other program ABCBS uses generates a loneliness score for pregnant members. Social isolation during pregnancy is a leading predictor of both perinatal depression and poor birth outcomes
  • These screenings are conducted over phone by CSC staff — not relying on a member to complete a form or log into a portal. The human-first model is why Pyx exceeds completion rate targets

ARKANSAS CONTEXT — WHY THIS MATTERS

4th highest preterm birth rate · high maternal mortality · rural access gaps — the conditions that make Pyx's engagement model non-optional

Arkansas ranks among the worst states in the country for maternal health outcomes. High preterm birth rates, significant rural transportation barriers, and limited prenatal provider access in many counties mean that reaching a pregnant ARHOME member with a standard reminder call is not enough. Pyx's model — food box milestone incentives + human CSC support + EPDS screening + SDoH navigation — is designed specifically for the conditions Arkansas members face. The program doesn't ask members to engage with a portal. It calls them, meets them where they are, and rewards care actions with something they need. That's why it works where standard outreach doesn't.

Talking Points — Plan Value

  • HEDIS PPC improvement is the measurable quality outcome this program is built to drive — every postpartum attestation Pyx documents is a data point ABCBS can use in quality reporting for the ARHOME population. This is not an engagement program that feeds a dashboard — it is a quality improvement mechanism
  • EPDS documentation at scale gives ABCBS a behavioral health dataset for the ARHOME maternal population that does not exist anywhere else. These are the members most at risk for postpartum depression claims in the 6–12 months after delivery — Pyx is the early warning system
  • The 1% YOY reduction target in preterm and low birth weight (per SOW Exhibit A) establishes a concrete birth outcomes benchmark — and the food box program addresses food insecurity (a clinical risk factor for preterm birth) as a direct intervention, not a side effect
  • SDoH documentation from Pyx — transportation, food, housing, safety — is the intelligence ABCBS needs for ARHOME equity reporting. Arkansas Medicaid is under increasing scrutiny on maternal health equity; Pyx generates the member-level SDOH data that demonstrates ABCBS is actively addressing root causes
  • Warm handoffs to ABCBS's Special Delivery program mean the most complex members identified by Pyx are escalated into clinical case management — Pyx and Special Delivery are the two-layer model for ARHOME maternal risk stratification

Talking Points — Loneliness & Distress Screening

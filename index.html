CRITICAL FINDING — 18–24 COHORT

EPDS average of 9.7 for youngest members — one point from the clinical depression flag, six weeks in

The 18–24 age group — the largest cohort in the program — averages an EPDS score of 9.7, just below the 10-point clinical flag for possible depression (13+ indicates probable depression). The overall program average of 7.7 is also elevated. These are pregnant women carrying significant depressive symptomatology that their prenatal provider has no clinical mechanism to detect between office visits. Pyx is the detection layer. Every EPDS score above 10 is a member who needs clinical follow-up — and Pyx is generating that documentation at scale, in real time, for ABCBS.

Talking Points — Clinical Screening

  • 78% EPDS completion in six weeks — exceeding the contracted 60% target — demonstrates that phone-based, human-first screening works for this population. Pregnant ARHOME members will complete an EPDS when a CSC member calls them; they will not log into a portal to do it
  • EPDS average of 7.7 with the 18–24 cohort at 9.7: these scores are not background noise. Members approaching or exceeding 10 are experiencing depressive symptoms during pregnancy that correlate with preterm birth, low birth weight, and postpartum complications. Pyx is identifying this cohort six weeks in
  • 94% PRAPARE completion (well above the 60% target) means Pyx has a near-complete SDoH profile on enrolled members — this is the documented social needs data ABCBS needs for ARHOME equity reporting
  • UCLA-3 of 4.3 establishes the social isolation baseline — the 18–24 cohort's 4.6 score, combined with their 9.7 EPDS, creates a compounded clinical risk profile that no claims data would surface

ARKANSAS CONTEXT — WHY THIS MATTERS

4th highest preterm birth rate · high maternal mortality · rural access gaps — the conditions that make Pyx's engagement model non-optional

Arkansas ranks among the worst states in the country for maternal health outcomes. High preterm birth rates, significant rural transportation barriers, and limited prenatal provider access in many counties mean that reaching a pregnant ARHOME member with a standard reminder call is not enough. Pyx's model — food box milestone incentives + human CSC support + EPDS screening + SDoH navigation — is designed specifically for the conditions Arkansas members face. The program doesn't ask members to engage with a portal. It calls them, meets them where they are, and rewards care actions with something they need. That's why it works where standard outreach doesn't.

Talking Points — Plan Value

  • HEDIS PPC improvement is the measurable quality outcome this program is built to drive — every postpartum attestation Pyx documents is a data point ABCBS can use in quality reporting for the ARHOME population. This is not an engagement program that feeds a dashboard — it is a quality improvement mechanism
  • EPDS documentation at scale gives ABCBS a behavioral health dataset for the ARHOME maternal population that does not exist anywhere else. These are the members most at risk for postpartum depression claims in the 6–12 months after delivery — Pyx is the early warning system
  • The 1% YOY reduction target in preterm and low birth weight (per SOW Exhibit A) establishes a concrete birth outcomes benchmark — and the food box program addresses food insecurity (a clinical risk factor for preterm birth) as a direct intervention, not a side effect
  • SDoH documentation from Pyx — transportation, food, housing, safety — is the intelligence ABCBS needs for ARHOME equity reporting.   • Warm handoffs to ABCBS's Special Delivery program mean the most complex members identified by Pyx are escalated into clinical case management — Pyx and Special Delivery are the two-layer model for ARHOME maternal risk stratification
